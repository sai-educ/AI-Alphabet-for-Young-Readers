How to Use Our AI Alphabet!

Ideas For Teachers, Tutors, Students, and Families

Welcome to our AI Alphabet for Young Readers!

Our goal is to provide teachers, tutors, students and families easy-to-read, kid-friendly definitions of words that generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) technologies have brought into our everyday lives in schools, homes and communities.

Our inspiration for creating this resource is based on a growing recognition that children as young as kindergarten and first grade need to begin developing AI literacy that includes understanding what artificial intelligence tools can and cannot do. As one commentator noted, it's important to teach youngsters that "technology is a helper, not a human" (Kwan, 2025).

Words to Know

Each page of the alphabet features a Words to Know definition of an AI word from A to Z. The words have been crowdsourced from curriculum frameworks, conversations with teachers and college students, and our own experiences with digital technologies and social media.

Each word has been defined by Estella Explainer Math Bot 2 (Estella AI), a chatbot we have fine-tuned to provide kid-friendly explanations of AI terms.

Things to Do

Every word in the Alphabet includes interactive Things to Do activities that readers can use to learn more about the term and the larger field of artificial intelligence while expanding their creativity through writing, designing, coding and inventing.

Teacher Resources

This resource is intended for teachers to help their young readers understand AI concepts in an engaging and age-appropriate way.

üìö

Teacher's Guide

This AI Alphabet provides a structured approach to introducing AI concepts to young learners. Each letter includes definitions crafted specifically for elementary school students, along with hands-on activities that reinforce learning through creativity and play.

üí° Tip: Start with familiar concepts like "Robot" or "Internet" before moving to more abstract ideas.

üé®

Activity Ideas

Each letter includes "Things to Do" activities that span multiple disciplines:

  • Creative writing and storytelling
  • Art and design projects
  • Problem-solving puzzles
  • Historical connections
  • Hands-on coding experiences
üìä

Assessment Ideas

Track student understanding through:

  • Vocabulary journals with illustrations
  • Creative projects demonstrating understanding
  • "Teach a Friend" activities
  • Building their own AI alphabet

Remember: The goal is to build AI literacy gradually and make learning enjoyable!
