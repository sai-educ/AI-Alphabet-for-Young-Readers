How to Use Our AI Alphabet!

Ideas For Teachers, Tutors, Students, and Families

Welcome to our AI Alphabet for Young Readers!

Our goal is to provide teachers, tutors, students and families easy-to-read, kid-friendly definitions of words that generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) technologies have brought into our everyday lives in schools, homes and communities.

Our inspiration for creating this resource is based on a growing recognition that children as young as kindergarten and first grade need to begin developing AI literacy that includes understanding what artificial intelligence tools can and cannot do. As one commentator noted, it's important to teach youngsters that "technology is a helper, not a human" (Kwan, 2025).

A key to AI literacy is for youngsters to begin recognizing and understanding the new vocabularies that are increasingly part of the emerging age of AI in schools and society. AI and social media-driven new vocabularies are referred to as "algospeak." Alex Aleksic, also known as @etymoloogynerd, notes how these words and concepts spread virally online so people use them without really fully understanding them (2025).

Words to Know

Each page of the alphabet features a Words to Know definition of an AI word from A to Z. The words have been crowdsourced from curriculum frameworks, conversations with teachers and college students, and our own experiences with digital technologies and social media.

Each word has been defined by Estella Explainer Math Bot 2 (Estella AI), a chatbot we have fine-tuned to provide kid-friendly explanations of AI terms. Estella AI is part of our Usable Math project and is available free online at ChatGPT.

Things to Do

Every word in the Alphabet includes interactive Things to Do activities that readers can use to learn more about the term and the larger field of artificial intelligence while expanding their creativity through writing, designing, coding and inventing.

Interactive activities include opportunities to write and draw stories, play games, learn about history, solve puzzles, develop math skills, and more. The interactive activities we have proposed for one AI word can be used with other AI words as well, greatly expanding the ways children and adults can use this AI alphabet.

Add to the Site and Build Your Own

Our AI Alphabet list is just a starting point; it is not intended to be comprehensive or definitive. We invite readers to contribute more words, develop their own definitions and explanations, design their own alphabets and pictionaries and continue to expand everyone's knowledge as AI evolves in schools and society.

Teacher Resources

This resource is intended for teachers to help their young readers understand AI concepts in an engaging and age-appropriate way.

Teacher's Guide

This AI Alphabet provides a structured approach to introducing AI concepts to young learners. Each letter includes definitions crafted specifically for elementary school students, along with hands-on activities that reinforce learning through creativity and play.

How to use: Start with familiar concepts like "Robot" or "Internet" before moving to more abstract ideas like "Algorithm" or "Neural Network."

Activity Worksheets

Each letter includes "Things to Do" activities that can be adapted for individual or group work. These activities span multiple disciplines including:

  • Creative writing and storytelling
  • Art and design projects
  • Problem-solving puzzles
  • Historical connections
  • Hands-on coding experiences

Assessment Ideas

Track student understanding through:

  • Vocabulary journals where students illustrate and define AI terms
  • Creative projects that demonstrate understanding
  • "Teach a Friend" activities where students explain concepts
  • Building their own AI alphabet with additional words

Remember: The goal is to build AI literacy gradually and make learning enjoyable!
